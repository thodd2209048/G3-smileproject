Overview

Oral health is fundamental to overall health and critical for the wellbeing of us all

Oral health is fundamental to overall health and critical for the wellbeing of us all. Good oral health is established with the right behaviors early in life. The European Academy of Paediatric Dentistry (EAPD) advises that twice daily toothbrushing with fluoride toothpaste can prevent caries and should be at the core of every child’s personal care routine.¹

Oral-B is committed to partnering with Dental Professionals to improve children’s oral health for life

Tools to Strengthen the Dental Team: Oral-B ‘s “Strong Teeth Make Strong Kids” (supported by the University of Leeds) and Test Drive programmes help set dental teams up for success with clinically proven resources to educate and support parents on how they can help their children develop the right oral health habits.

Achieving good plaque control presents a significant challenge

The average 5-year-old using a manual toothbrush may effectively brush only 25% of their dentition, with the lingual tooth surfaces being the most commonly missed areas.¹

80% of toddlers object to tooth brushing² • 61% of under 5s brush their teeth unsupervised by an adult from the age of 3³

The Scientific Evidence of Strong Teeth Strong Kids

Oral-B oscillating rotating toothbrush technology is supported by a robust peer-reviewed body of scientific evidence. Oscillating-Rotating technology’s efficacy, gentleness and safety helps children and their parents establish effective brushing behaviors to achieve positive oral health outcomes for life.
